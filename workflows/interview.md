# User Interview Guide Workflow

## Input
A hypothesis to test or a problem space to explore. If unclear, ask: "What do you believe is true about your users that you want to validate or challenge?"

## What makes a great interview
- Ask about the past, not the future: "Tell me about the last time..." not "Would you ever..."
- Never pitch your solution — you're here to learn, not confirm
- Silence is productive — let users fill it, don't rescue them
- Follow the energy: when someone pauses, sighs, or hedges — dig there
- One question at a time — never stack two questions in one

## Opening (2 min)
"Thanks for your time. I'm trying to understand [problem space] — I'm not pitching anything today. There are no right or wrong answers; I just want to hear about your experience. Can you tell me a bit about [relevant aspect of their role or situation]?"

## Core questions (25–35 min)

**1. Set the scene**
"Tell me about the last time you had to [situation related to hypothesis]. Walk me through what happened."

**2. Uncover the job**
"What were you actually trying to accomplish when that happened?"

**3. Current solution**
"What did you do? How do you handle this today?"

**4. Friction**
"What's the most frustrating part of that process?"

**5. Stakes**
"What happens if you get this wrong? What's on the line?"

**6. Workarounds**
"Have you tried other approaches? What made you try those?"

"Is there anything you expected me to ask that I didn't? Anyone else I should be talking to?"
